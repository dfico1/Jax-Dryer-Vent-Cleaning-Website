Safety

Very. The U.S. Fire Administration reports that clogged dryer vents cause approximately 15,000 house fires every year, resulting in deaths, injuries, and hundreds of millions of dollars in property damage. Lint is highly flammable, and when it builds up inside the vent, even a small spark from the heating element can ignite it. A clean vent is one of the simplest ways to protect your home and family.

Watch for these warning signs:

  • Clothes take more than one cycle to dry fully
  • Clothes or the dryer itself feel unusually hot after a cycle
  • You notice a burning smell when the dryer is running
  • The laundry room feels more humid than usual
  • The exterior vent flap doesn't open when the dryer is on
  • It's been more than a year since your last cleaning

If you notice any of these, stop using the dryer until it's been inspected.

You can remove lint from the lint trap and clean a few feet of duct with a brush kit from the hardware store — but this only addresses the first section of the vent. Most of the lint buildup occurs deeper in the run, around bends, and at the exterior termination point. A professional rotary brush system reaches the full length of the vent and measures airflow before and after to confirm it's clear. DIY cleaning often leaves the most dangerous buildup untouched.

How Often

The Chimney Safety Institute of America recommends once per year for most households. With Northeast Florida's high-humidity climate, lint compacts more quickly than in drier regions. Many homes benefit from cleaning every 6 months. You may also need more frequent cleaning if you have a large family doing heavy laundry, pets that shed, or a dryer vent run longer than 25 feet.

Yes, and sooner than most people expect. New construction in Jacksonville (Nocatee, St. Johns, Ponte Vedra) often features longer vent runs due to slab-on-grade layouts. These longer runs trap lint faster than shorter ones, and construction debris can also partially block the vent from day one. We recommend a first cleaning within the first year of moving in.
